Academic Research
Bias Detection in Large Language Models using Fairness Metrics
MSc in Artificial Intelligence | The University of Edinburgh | 2024-2025

Research Focus: Applied systematic evaluation frameworks and root cause analysis to identify and quantify bias in complex language model systems. The thesis explored fairness metrics including demographic parity, equalized odds, and disparate impact to detect and measure bias across different model architectures and training datasets.

Methodology: This research adopts an experimental and exploratory design, focusing on evaluating and mitigating biases in LLM models. The research workflow is depicted in a visual diagram in 3.1. The methodology utilizes three pre-selected datasets: Amazon Engineering, IMDB, and Voices of the Poor. These datasets encompass the technical, entertainment, and socioeconomic domains, incorporating data from the National Health Service (NHS). Three established natural language processing (NLP) models —DistilBERT, Electra, and RoBERTa —are applied to each dataset. This approach enables systematic evaluation of model performance and identification of potential biases. The bias evaluation step identifies three types of bias: misrepresentation, bias amplification, and proxy discrimination. Fairness metrics such as equalized odds and demographic parity are assessed in conjunction with quantitative metrics, including accuracy and F1-score. These results provide an initial basis for evaluating the fairness of the models in their unaltered state. To mitigate the biases that have been identified, the next step is to implement debiasing techniques. These methods, selected based on their ability to address specific aspects of bias. The trade-offs between task performance and debiasing effectiveness are then assessed by reevaluating the models using task-specific and fairness metrics. Sensitive terms, including ’rich,’ ’homeless,’ and ’poor,’ are assigned to broader contextual categories through an ontological framework. This process enables the identification of persistent biases within the datasets and improves interpretability. The workflow concludes with a summary of key outcomes: reduced model biases, increased clarity regarding the balance between performance and fairness, and explicit attention to ethical considerations in the analysis of socioeconomically sensitive texts.

Key Contributions: Demonstrated rigorous analytical techniques for risk assessment and data integrity, providing frameworks applicable to ensuring fairness in AI-driven business systems, customer analytics, and automated decision-making platforms.

Relevance to Professional Work: The systematic evaluation methodologies and fairness considerations directly apply to ensuring data integrity in analytics projects, bias detection in customer segmentation, and ethical AI implementation in enterprise systems.
