Featured Project

Invoice Clearance &
Payment Reconciliation Automation

Automated invoice processing for 10,000+ monthly transactions from 50+ vendors,
reducing billing discrepancies by 35% and shortening payment cycles from 15 to 4 days.

The Problem

The organisation was processing over 10,000 invoices monthly from 50+ third-party vendors for EV electricity bills and infrastructure costs. The manual process was causing significant operational and financial challenges:

  • Manual data entry from paper and PDF invoices
  • High rate of billing discrepancies requiring rework
  • Payment cycles averaging 15 days, causing vendor dissatisfaction
  • No visibility into invoice status for vendors or internal teams
  • Late payment penalties accumulating due to processing delays
  • Finance team spending 80% of time on manual data entry and validation
  • Difficulty scaling to support rapid network growth

My Role

I led the end-to-end automation of the invoice clearance and payment reconciliation process. My responsibilities included:

  • Process Analysis: Mapped current invoice processing workflow and identified bottlenecks
  • Solution Design: Designed automated validation and approval workflows
  • System Integration: Integrated with SAP for financial reconciliation
  • Dashboard Development: Built visibility dashboards for stakeholders
  • Vendor Onboarding: Worked with 50+ vendors to transition to automated process
  • Change Management: Trained internal teams on new processes

Approach & Methodology

Step 1: Current State Analysis

Analysed the existing manual invoice processing workflow:

❌ Before Automation:
• Manual data entry from 50+ vendors
• Paper and PDF invoices
• 15-day average payment cycle
• 35% discrepancy rate
• No status visibility
• Late payment penalties

Step 2: Automated Workflow Design

Designed an automated invoice processing workflow:

Step 1: Invoice Submission
Vendors submit invoices via vendor portal with standardised format
Step 2: Automated Validation
System validates against PO, rates, quantities, and tax calculations
Step 3: Exception Handling
Discrepancies automatically flagged for review with vendor
Step 4: Approval Workflow
Multi-level approval based on invoice amount and category
Step 5: SAP Integration
Automatic posting to SAP FICO for payment processing
Step 6: Vendor Notification
Automatic status updates and payment confirmation to vendors

Step 3: Validation Rules Implementation

Implemented automated validation rules to catch discrepancies early:

  • Price Validation: Matches invoice rates against contract rates
  • Quantity Validation: Verifies billed quantities against delivery records
  • Tax Validation: Checks GST/VAT calculations and rates
  • Duplicate Detection: Identifies potentially duplicate invoices
  • Vendor Verification: Confirms vendor details against master data

Step 4: Dashboard Development

Created real-time dashboards for different stakeholders:

  • Finance Dashboard: Invoice aging, payment cycle trends, discrepancy analysis
  • Vendor Dashboard: Submission status, payment schedule, dispute resolution
  • Leadership Dashboard: Cost trends, vendor performance, cash flow impact

Step 5: Vendor Onboarding

Managed transition of 50+ vendors to automated process:

  • Provided training and documentation to each vendor
  • Set up vendor accounts in the portal
  • Conducted pilot with top 5 vendors before full rollout
  • Established support process for vendor queries
Technologies Used:
SAP Power Automate ServiceNow Excel Advanced Power BI SQL

Challenges Overcome

  • Vendor Diversity: 50+ vendors with different invoice formats and processes. Standardised submission format while accommodating variations.
  • Integration Complexity: Successfully integrated automated workflows with SAP FICO despite complex data mapping requirements.
  • Change Resistance: Finance team accustomed to manual processes. Addressed through training, support, and demonstrating time savings.
  • Data Quality: Historical data had inconsistencies. Implemented data cleansing before automation.
  • Exception Handling: Designed intuitive exception workflow for discrepancies requiring human review.

Outcome & Impact

  • Billing discrepancies reduced by 35% through automated validation
  • Payment cycles shortened from 15 to 4 days (73% improvement)
  • Late payment penalties eliminated through automated processing
  • Finance team manual effort reduced by 70%
  • 100% invoice status visibility for vendors and internal teams
  • Improved vendor satisfaction with faster, predictable payments
  • Scalable process supporting network growth without additional headcount

Key Learnings

This project taught me that automation is not just about technology—it's about process redesign. Simply digitising a bad process creates a faster bad process. We needed to redesign the workflow from scratch, eliminating unnecessary steps and building validation at each stage.

I also learned that vendor engagement is critical. The most sophisticated automation fails if vendors can't or won't use it. Investing time in vendor onboarding and support paid off in adoption and compliance.
