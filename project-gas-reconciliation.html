Featured Project

Gas Purchase vs. Sales
Reconciliation System

Led investigation into an 8% discrepancy between gas purchased and sold across 500+ stations,
recovering £2.3M annually and reducing discrepancies to under 2%.

The Problem

The organisation discovered an 8% discrepancy between the volume of gas purchased and the volume sold across 500+ stations. This represented significant financial leakage and operational inefficiency. Key challenges included:

  • 8% unaccounted gas losses across the network
  • No clear understanding of root causes
  • Manual reconciliation processes across multiple systems
  • Data inconsistencies between purchase and sales records
  • Inability to pinpoint which stations or regions had the highest losses
  • Financial impact estimated at over £2M annually

My Role

I led the end-to-end investigation and reconciliation effort. My responsibilities included:

  • Data Analysis: Analysed purchase and sales data from 500+ stations
  • Root Cause Analysis: Conducted systematic investigation using 5 Whys methodology
  • Process Mapping: Mapped the end-to-end gas measurement and reporting process
  • Solution Design: Developed automated reconciliation workflows
  • Implementation: Rolled out validation rules and monitoring dashboards
  • Stakeholder Communication: Presented findings and recommendations to leadership

Approach & Methodology

Step 1: Data Collection & Validation

Gathered gas purchase and sales data from multiple sources:

  • Purchase data: Supplier invoices, delivery records, SAP procurement modules
  • Sales data: Station meter readings, billing systems, customer consumption records
  • Master data: Station details, meter calibration records, location information

Validated data quality and identified gaps or inconsistencies.

Step 2: Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys)

Conducted systematic root cause analysis to identify the underlying causes of the discrepancy:

1 Why is there an 8% discrepancy?
Because gas purchase records don't match sales records.
2 Why don't purchase and sales records match?
Because meter readings are inconsistent and reporting has delays.
3 Why are meter readings inconsistent?
Because some meters are not calibrated regularly and manual reading errors occur.
4 Why are meters not calibrated regularly?
Because there is no automated calibration tracking or alert system.
5 Why is there no automated tracking system?
Because processes were designed for smaller scale and not updated during growth.
🔍 Root Causes Identified:
1. Meter calibration issues across 15% of stations
2. Manual reading errors due to lack of validation
3. Reporting delays between field teams and finance
4. Theft at 3 high-loss stations
5. No automated reconciliation process

Step 3: Impact Quantification

Quantified the financial impact of each root cause:

  • Meter calibration issues: £1.2M annual loss
  • Manual reading errors: £0.6M annual loss
  • Reporting delays: £0.3M annual loss
  • Theft: £0.2M annual loss
  • Total estimated annual loss: £2.3M

Step 4: Solution Design

Developed a multi-pronged solution addressing each root cause:

  • Automated Reconciliation: Built automated workflows to match purchase and sales records daily
  • Validation Rules: Implemented automated checks for meter reading anomalies
  • Calibration Tracking: Created system to track meter calibration schedules and alerts
  • Reporting Automation: Automated data collection from field teams
  • Security Measures: Implemented additional controls at high-theft stations

Step 5: Dashboard Development

Created Power BI dashboards for monitoring:

  • Daily reconciliation status by station
  • Top stations with highest discrepancies
  • Meter calibration status and upcoming due dates
  • Trend analysis of discrepancies over time
  • Financial impact tracking
Technologies Used:
SAP FICO Excel Power Query Automated Reconciliation Workflows Power BI SQL

Challenges Overcome

  • Data Fragmentation: Purchase and sales data existed in different systems with different formats. Created integration layer to standardise data.
  • Stakeholder Resistance: Field teams were initially resistant to additional reporting requirements. Addressed through training and demonstrating value.
  • Theft Identification: Sensitive issue requiring discreet investigation and coordinated action with security teams.
  • Legacy Systems: Some stations had older meters that couldn't be automated. Developed manual validation processes for these cases.

Outcome & Impact

  • £2.3M annual recovery through reduced discrepancies
  • Discrepancies reduced from 8% to under 2%
  • Automated daily reconciliation replacing manual monthly process
  • 100% meter calibration compliance achieved through automated tracking
  • Reduced manual effort by 70% for finance team
  • Improved data accuracy across all 500+ stations

Key Learnings

This project reinforced the power of systematic root cause analysis. The 5 Whys methodology helped us move beyond surface-level symptoms to identify true root causes. Without this structured approach, we might have implemented quick fixes that wouldn't address underlying issues.

I also learned that financial impact data is critical for stakeholder buy-in. Quantifying the £2.3M annual loss made it easy to secure resources and leadership support for the solution.
