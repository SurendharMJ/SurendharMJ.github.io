Featured Project

Customer Churn Analysis
& Retention Strategy

Analysed 10,000+ EV subscriber records across 2 years, identified key churn drivers,
and implemented strategies that reduced churn by 22% and increased satisfaction by 18 points.

The Problem

The EV subscription business was experiencing rising customer churn rates, but there was no clear understanding of why customers were leaving. Key challenges included:

  • No visibility into churn patterns or root causes
  • Manual analysis of customer data was time-consuming and incomplete
  • Competitors were entering the market with aggressive pricing
  • Customer retention efforts were reactive rather than proactive
  • No data-driven framework for retention strategy

My Role

I led the end-to-end churn analysis and retention strategy development. My responsibilities included:

  • Data Collection & Cleaning: Gathered and prepared 10,000+ subscriber records
  • Exploratory Analysis: Identified patterns and correlations in churn behaviour
  • Segmentation: Developed customer segments based on usage patterns and demographics
  • Root Cause Analysis: Conducted deep-dive into churn drivers
  • Strategy Recommendation: Presented findings and recommended retention initiatives
  • Impact Measurement: Tracked and reported on strategy effectiveness

Approach & Methodology

Step 1: Data Collection & Preparation

Gathered subscriber data from multiple sources including CRM, billing systems, and usage logs. The dataset included:

  • 10,000+ EV subscriber records
  • 2 years of historical data
  • Variables: subscription tenure, usage patterns, billing history, location, competitor presence, support tickets

Cleaned and transformed data using Python (pandas) and SQL, handling missing values and standardising formats.

Step 2: Exploratory Data Analysis

Conducted initial analysis to understand churn patterns:

  • Calculated overall churn rate and trends over time
  • Analysed churn by region, subscription type, and customer tenure
  • Identified seasonal patterns in churn behaviour
  • Created visualisations in Power BI to explore relationships

Step 3: Customer Segmentation

Developed customer segments based on:

  • Usage Patterns: Heavy users vs light users
  • Geographic Location: Urban vs suburban vs rural
  • Competitor Density: Areas with 0-1, 2-3, or 3+ competing charging points
  • Tenure: New (0-6 months), medium (6-18 months), long-term (18+ months)

Step 4: Root Cause Analysis

Identified key churn drivers through statistical analysis:

🔍 Key Insight Discovered: Customers in areas with three or more competing charging points were three times more likely to churn than those in areas with limited competition.

Additional findings included:

  • Price sensitivity was highest among light users
  • Churn peaked at the 6-month and 12-month marks
  • Customers with unresolved support tickets had 2x higher churn risk

Step 5: Strategy Development

Based on the analysis, developed targeted retention strategies:

  • Loyalty Pricing: Introduced tiered loyalty discounts for high-risk segments
  • Competitive Response: Matched competitor pricing in high-density areas
  • Proactive Outreach: Automated engagement campaigns at key churn risk periods (6 and 12 months)
  • Support Improvement: Prioritised ticket resolution for high-risk customers

Step 6: Implementation & Tracking

Worked with marketing and operations teams to implement strategies. Created a real-time churn tracking dashboard to monitor:

  • Overall churn rate trends
  • Segment-specific churn performance
  • Strategy effectiveness by region
  • Customer satisfaction scores
Technologies Used:

Challenges Overcome

  • Data Quality: Inconsistent data across multiple sources required significant cleaning and validation.
  • Competitor Data: Had to manually gather and validate competitor presence data across regions.
  • Stakeholder Buy-in: Needed to build a compelling data-driven case to convince leadership to invest in loyalty pricing.
  • Implementation Coordination: Worked across marketing, operations, and finance teams to implement strategies.

Outcome & Impact

  • Churn reduced by 22% within six months of implementation
  • Customer satisfaction scores increased by 18 points
  • Loyalty pricing adjustments implemented in high-competition areas
  • Proactive retention campaigns automated at key risk periods
  • Data-driven culture established for ongoing customer analytics
  • £[X] annual revenue protected through reduced churn

Key Learnings

This project taught me that data alone isn't enough—the real value comes from translating insights into action. The discovery that competition density was the strongest churn predictor was surprising, but acting on it with loyalty pricing made the difference.

I also learned the importance of customer segmentation. One-size-fits-all retention strategies are ineffective; different customer segments have different needs and churn drivers. Tailored approaches deliver better results.
